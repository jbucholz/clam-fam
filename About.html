Our Mission

Uniting researchers, agencies, and advocates to restore the lifeblood of Atlantic slope rivers.

Protecting a Hidden Heritage

Freshwater mussels are the "silent engineers" of our ecosystems. A single mussel can filter up to 10 gallons of water a day, yet they are among the most endangered species groups in North America.

The our group was established to bridge the gap between isolated research and basin-wide action. We focus on the unique challenges of Atlantic slope drainages—from the Delaware to the Altamaha.

Research

Standardizing surveying protocols and sharing demographic data across state lines.

Restoration

Coordinating augmentation and reintroduction efforts for high-priority species.

Education

Raising public awareness through community science and "Shell-ebrity Makeover" events.

We believe restoration is fundamentally a community initiative

Our community outreach program invites the public to learn about mussel tagging for river restorations.
