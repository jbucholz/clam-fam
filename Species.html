Family: Unionidae

*Elliptio complanata*

Eastern Elliptio

Conservation Status

Currently Secure

Key Characteristics

  • Trapezoidal shell shape
  • Purple nacre (usually)
  • Prominent posterior ridge

Host Fish

American Eel (*Anguilla rostrata*), Brook Trout, and others.

Overview

The Eastern Elliptio is perhaps the most ubiquitous freshwater mussel along the Atlantic Slope. Found from Georgia to Canada, it plays a critical role in nutrient cycling and water filtration in both small streams and large river systems.

Identification

While highly variable in form, *E. complanata* is typically characterized by its compressed, trapezoidal shell. The interior nacre is most famously a deep "royal" purple, though white and salmon variations occur in certain watersheds.
